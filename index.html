Current & arriving guests

Guest information.

Everything you need while you're staying with us — full park policies, site standards, and seasonal notices.

Lazy Acres is a privately owned, month-to-month park (daily and weekly stays welcome). Only approved RVs are allowed, and we reserve the right to refuse service to anyone who won't comply with park policies. All reservations are non-refundable and non-transferable.

Your stay & site

  • Quiet hours 10 PM–7 AM. Be considerate at all other hours, too.
  • Check-out is 11:00 AM unless approved by management.
  • Limit 4 guests per site (2 adults + 2 children). Extra adult $5/night, extra child $3/night for daily and weekly — call the office about month-to-month.
  • Keep your site tidy. Picnic tables stay on the slab provided — please don't move them.
  • Free Wi-Fi ("Lazy Acres WiFi") is a courtesy, not part of your rate, and may not reach every site. The password is given at check-in.

Trash & fires

  • Bag all trash and place it inside the dumpster. No trash cans left outside at sites.
  • No construction material, wood, appliances, or furniture in the dumpsters — there are free dumpsters in town for those (ask the office).
  • Campfires in approved fire rings only. Never place grills on picnic tables.

Pets

  • Leashed at all times (6 ft max, in hand). Sitting outside, a pet may be tied to your RV or a stake. Only foldable portable fences — no dog houses.
  • Clean up after your pet immediately and dispose of waste in a receptacle.
  • Proof of vaccination required for all pets.
  • No aggressive dogs of any breed. Rottweilers, Pit Bulls, Chows, and Dobermans are restricted.
  • Service dogs must be registered at the office with documentation and approved by management.
  • No pets in the bathhouse or laundromat; don't wash pet bedding in the washers.

Driving & parking

  • Observe the posted speed limit and don't cut across other sites.
  • No through traffic — please don't use the park as a cut-through.
  • Park on your slab, not the grass, and keep cords clear of the mowers.
  • Motorcycles for transportation only (factory parts). Golf carts welcome; no four-wheeling.
  • Limit 2 vehicles per site — a boat, golf cart, or recreational vehicle by office permission.

Bathhouse & laundry

  • No smoking in the bathhouse, and don't leave clothes unattended.
  • Restrooms are gender-specific — violators will be asked to leave immediately.
  • Trash goes in the dumpsters, never in the laundry or bathhouse.
  • H.E. washers and dryers — please use H.E. detergent.

Safety & liability

  • Children under 18 must be supervised by an adult; parents are responsible for any damage or injury caused by their children.
  • No firearms, illegal drugs, or violence tolerated. Bring any complaints to the office right away.
  • Standing water is not for swimming, wading, or fishing. No lifeguard on duty — do not use.
  • RV washing: no charge to wash your own RV; professional washing must be approved by the office.
  • Not responsible for damage or loss to you or your property while in the park.
Winter — mandatory

Heated water hoses required

Every site must use a heated water hose through the freezing season. Any RV without one will have its water turned off, and water is restored only with the office's permission.

If your water is turned off and you reconnect it without permission, you'll be asked to vacate the park. Frozen lines have cost the park thousands of dollars — and they're completely preventable.

— Albert Bell, Owner · via Vina "Carol" George, Manager

Keeping every site clean and orderly protects the whole park and keeps mowing and maintenance easy. These standards apply year-round (rules include, but aren't limited to, the following):

Around your RV

  • No trash in the fire pit or around/under your RV. Don't leave trash bags out; keep clutter cleared.
  • No buckets, crates, random wood, or debris. Neatly stacked firewood by your fire pit is fine. No ladders left on the ground.
  • Limit storage bins under the RV — nothing broken or unsightly. No outside refrigerators; store toolboxes in your RV storage area.
  • No inside furniture used outdoors. Old boxes and packages must be removed, not stacked against the RV.
  • Approved tents are for lawn furniture and barbeques, not storage.

Equipment & hookups

  • Factory RV skirting only — no boxes, plastic, tarps, or hay.
  • Replace rusted propane bottles. Limit two 100 lb tanks or three 30 lb tanks per site.
  • TV cables off the ground; sewer lines elevated on a proper sewer-hose stand.
  • Remove unused blocks and clean under your steps. Check with the office before building steps.
  • No car cleaners, brake fluid, oil, rags, or jugs left out — store them in your RV storage area.

Décor & flags

  • Take down seasonal decorations once the holiday's over. Yard décor is welcome, but replace anything broken, rusted, or missing parts.
  • American/military flags limited to 3'×5' on proper pole holders mounted on your RV (not in the ground). University flags limited to 28"×40".
  • You're responsible for weeds and grass around your décor and dog fences.

Vehicles & mowing

  • Limit 2 vehicles per site; a boat, golf cart, or recreational vehicle by office permission. One utility trailer per site, approved by management. Don't use a neighbor's space.
  • Move outdoor rugs while mowing — otherwise that area becomes your responsibility.
  • Slow down, follow the traffic pattern, and stay off the grass.
